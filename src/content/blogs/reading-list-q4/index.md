A short, opinionated list. No summaries — if a paper interests you, read it
yourself. The point of a reading list is the *selection*.

## Papers

1. **A Mathematical Framework for Transformer Circuits** — Elhage et al. The
   paper that made me stop being scared of attention.
2. **In-context Learning and Induction Heads** — Olsson et al. Read this
   immediately after the previous one.
3. **Toy Models of Superposition** — Elhage et al. The clearest paper on why
   features and neurons aren&apos;t the same thing.

## Essays

- *The Bitter Lesson* — Sutton. I disagree with the framing more every year and
  reread it anyway.
- *Worse Is Better* — Gabriel. Not about ML. Entirely about ML.

## Books

- *Information Theory, Inference, and Learning Algorithms* — MacKay. Free
  online. Has aged perfectly.

That&apos;s it. The shorter the list, the more carefully it was chosen.
